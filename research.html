Research & Design Reasoning

This document outlines the research assumptions, constraints, and evaluative reasoning behind the Latt project. It focuses on the rationale for design decisions, rather than the final output.

← Back to Project Overview

Research Focus

How can users maintain awareness and control over recurring digital subscriptions, while minimizing cognitive load and supporting trust during financial interactions?

Scope & Approach

This project used comparative analysis of existing subscription products to identify common friction points, failure patterns, and trust issues.

The goal was to understand how and why users lose awareness and confidence in managing subscriptions over time.

Key Assumptions & Open Questions

Assumptions

  • Users lose track of subscriptions due to cognitive overload, not lack of interest
  • Recurring payments can lead to passive financial loss if not monitored
  • Trust in financial products is fragile, especially during onboarding and failure states
  • Familiar interaction patterns reduce hesitation and errors

Open Questions

  • What level of transparency is needed to build and maintain user trust?
  • How much subscription detail is helpful before it becomes overwhelming?
  • Would users be comfortable with limited email scanning if value is demonstrated first?

User Mental Model

Users typically do not think of subscriptions as ongoing commitments. Instead, they experience them as background processes that fade from attention once the initial value is obtained.

  • Primary Goal: Understand what they are paying for
  • Constraint: Limited time and attention
  • Failure Mode: Forgetting → passive financial loss
  • Recovery Need: Visibility and easy management

Trust, Privacy & Ethical Considerations

Financial data access is a high-anxiety interaction. Users are skeptical of applications that request permissions before demonstrating clear value.

Design Principle: Trust is built progressively through transparency and user control.
  • Explain value before requesting permissions
  • Provide clear justification for each data access
  • Give users control over when and how their data is used
  • Avoid hidden or automatic background processes

Information Architecture Rationale

The interface prioritizes subscription visibility and clarity over feature discovery. Users should immediately understand their financial commitments without unnecessary navigation.

  • Dashboard focuses on active subscriptions and upcoming payments
  • Alerts are contextual and actionable
  • Subscription management is the primary function; discovery is secondary

Interaction Design Principles

  • Use familiar patterns to reduce cognitive effort
  • Make destructive actions (e.g., cancellation) visually distinct and confirm before execution
  • Ensure all errors are explainable and recoverable
  • Provide clear next steps during and after failure states

Alternative Approaches Considered

Full Bank Integration

Not pursued due to high trust barriers and user concerns about data misuse.

Manual Subscription Entry

Not pursued because it relies on user memory, which is the same failure point the project aims to address.

Evaluation Plan

The design would be evaluated through usability testing, focusing on:

  • Time and ease of identifying active subscriptions
  • Clarity and recoverability during failure and error states
  • User confidence in managing or cancelling subscriptions
  • Ability to recover from errors without external assistance

Summary

This project emphasized that trust and clarity are most critical during moments of uncertainty or failure. Designing for these scenarios leads to more reliable and user-friendly experiences.

The assumptions and principles outlined here directly informed interface prioritization, failure handling, and onboarding design.
