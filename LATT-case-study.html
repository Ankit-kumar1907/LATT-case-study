Context & Problem

Modern subscription systems rely on recurring payments that often continue unnoticed. Over time, users lose visibility into what they are paying for and why.

The issue is not forgetfulness alone, but a mismatch between user intent and system persistence.

Key Insight Most subscription losses occur passively through system behavior, not deliberate user choice.

Study & Research Overview

Prior to interface design, the focus was on understanding how users experience subscriptions over time — particularly around trust, attention, and financial anxiety.

Research Question How can users maintain control over recurring subscriptions without increasing anxiety or compromising trust?
Evidence Basis

Insights were informed by secondary UX research, comparative analysis of existing subscription management tools, and behavioral economics literature on passive financial loss and attention decay.

  • Subscriptions fade from attention after initial value is gained
  • Recurring payments create invisible financial loss
  • Trust drops when payment details are requested before value is shown
  • Familiar patterns reduce hesitation and cognitive load

This overview summarizes key insights only. A separate research document details assumptions, constraints, ethical considerations, and how these insights informed design decisions.

Deliberate Non-Goals

  • No automatic cancellation to prevent accidental harm
  • No persuasive upgrade nudges or dark patterns
  • No bank-first onboarding before value demonstration

Failure States & Risk Handling

Payment and network failures represent moments of peak anxiety. These were treated as primary interactions rather than edge cases.

Clear state visibility and messaging reassure users that their money remains safe, even when systems fail.

Failure Assumptions
  • Network instability is common in real-world mobile use
  • Users fear double charges more than delayed confirmation
  • Ambiguous states cause unsafe retries and trust erosion
Failure as a Design Input

Rather than treating errors as edge cases, failure scenarios were modeled as first-class user journeys and designed with the same rigor as successful payment flows.

Reflection & Learnings

This project reinforced that trust is built most clearly during uncertainty. Designing for breakdowns — not just success — leads to systems users can rely on.
